The Digital Standard is a framework of principles for companies and manufacturers to evaluate how their product or service represents people’s expectations and values. Our goal is to ensure these values are adopted as fundamental practice within the digital marketplace.

LEARN HOW TO USE THE DIGITAL STANDARD

Security

Products should be built to be secure. Electronics and software-based products must implement security best practices to reduce the risks from attackers, malware, and other threats.

Documentation

Privacy

Products should preserve consumer privacy. Consumer information that is collected by any product or service should be safeguarded and kept private.

Documentation

Ownership

Products should protect the idea of ownership. When consumers buy products, they should be able to alter, fix, or resell them.

Documentation

Governance

Companies should act ethically. Their products should be designed to help protect freedom of expression, address accessibility, and combat harassment.

Documentation

How to use The Digital Standard

The Digital Standard is built upon guiding values that are essential to building consumer confidence in technology. Each principle within The Digital Standard is presented with supporting criteria, indicators and methodologies to guide product teams, researchers and testers with evaluations.

The Digital Standard encourages manufacturers to:

Apply principles as fundamental features of building and designing products and services

Test and evaluate products with the appropriate methodologies outlined in the documentation prior to release

Immediately resolve any potential conflicts that products might have with the criteria and indicators outlined

Why does this matter?

The pace of technology is exciting and brings greater convenience to our lives, but also carries with it new problems as companies build products and services that don’t always reflect consumers’ best interests. Independent product testing has historically focused on how easy products and services are to use and their safety, quality, and cost. Today, such testing must also incorporate the individual and collective effects of products and services on privacy, fairness, and user control. The Digital Standard is designed to address these changing expectations, collaborate with key stakeholders to help define what is considered best practice and market-leading conduct, and influence the creation behind products and services before they are built and distributed in mass.
